# Breathing techniques — reference for the mizu coaching skill

Load this when starting a guided session. Use the exact counts below; do not
invent variations. These are widely-taught patterns, not medical prescriptions —
this is a wellness practice, not care. General cautions: if the member is pregnant,
has a respiratory or cardiovascular condition, or feels dizzy, keep breaths gentle
and unforced, shorten holds, and tell them to stop and breathe normally if
lightheaded. Never push a hold that feels strained.

## Match the moment → technique

| State / goal | Technique | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Anxious, wired, can't settle | Extended exhale, or 4-7-8 | A longer exhale than inhale shifts toward the calming (parasympathetic) side |
| Acute spike of stress, need relief fast | Physiological sigh (2–3 rounds) | Fastest reliable down-shift; good when there's no time for a full session |
| Need steady focus before deep work | Box breathing 4-4-4-4 | Even, balanced pace; steadies without sedating |
| Reset between meetings (2 min) | Coherent breathing ~5 in / 5 out | Simple, sustainable, no holds — easy to keep for a couple minutes |
| Winding down for sleep | 4-7-8 (slow), then just long exhales | Emphasizes the exhale and lengthens it toward rest |

## The patterns (exact counts)

**Box breathing — 4-4-4-4.** Inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4. Equal on all four
sides. Steadying and focusing. This is the pattern MizuMind's Rhythmic Pulse / box
work uses; if the member wants audio + pacing visuals, hand them the app deep-link.

**Coherent (resonant) breathing — ~5 in / 5 out.** No holds. About 5–6 breaths per
minute. The easiest to sustain for several minutes; good default for "just help me
settle."

**Extended exhale — 4 in / 6 out** (or 4 / 8 as they relax). No holds. Make the
exhale longer than the inhale. Reliable, gentle down-regulation.

**4-7-8 — inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8.** Strong down-shift; good for winding down.
Start with a shorter hold if it feels like too much; the ratio (short in, long out)
matters more than hitting 7 exactly. Only a few rounds — it's potent.

**Physiological sigh.** Two inhales through the nose (a normal breath, then a short
second sip to top off), followed by a long slow exhale through the mouth. Repeat
2–3 times. Best for an acute stress spike.

## Coaching the count out loud

Speak the count plainly and briefly — you are a metronome, not a narrator:

> Inhale, two, three, four. Hold, two, three, four. Exhale, two, three, four.
> Hold, two, three, four. Again.

Give 4–6 cycles for a reset; more if they asked for longer. Between cues, leave the
line quiet — don't fill every beat with words. Close by inviting them to notice how
they feel now, then offer the journal reflection (see SKILL.md step 4).
